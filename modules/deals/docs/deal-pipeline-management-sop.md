# Deal Pipeline Management
## SOP: Deal Pipeline Management - Sub Process

## Purpose

To ensure visibility, consistency, and control across all sales opportunities, from initial enquiry to course delivery, using a structured pipeline and stage management system.

## Step 1 - Deal Creation and Initial Qualification

- **Trigger**
  - New lead qualifies as a potential opportunity.
- **Action**
  - Create a new deal in the CRM.
  - Use consistent naming convention:
    `P - [Company] - [Location] - [Course/Contact]`
  - Assign the correct Account and Contact.
- **Classification**
  - Classify as New Business or Existing Business.
- **Mandatory fields to populate**
  - Number of attendees (estimate)
  - Product Type (Public / Private / Other)
  - Course Code (if applicable)
  - Quoted Amount (projected revenue)
- **Notes**
  - Attach any initial notes collected during the enquiry.

## Step 2 - Deal Stage Progression

Each deal must move through the CRM pipeline stages in order:

### Stage: New
- **Description:** Deal created, not yet qualified.
- **Action required:** Confirm details and set initial task.

### Stage: Qualified
- **Description:** Initial call/email made.
- **Action required:** Add notes, confirm interest and requirements.

### Stage: Ready to Quote
- **Description:** Info confirmed.
- **Action required:** Click "Ready to Quote" to generate quote.

### Stage: Quote Sent
- **Description:** Quote emailed.
- **Action required:** Record send date and set a follow-up task.

### Stage: Awaiting PO
- **Description:** Verbal or soft approval.
- **Action required:** Await signed PO or written confirmation.

### Stage: Course Booked
- **Description:** Dates and logistics confirmed.
- **Action required:** Enter final booking info and assign confirmed trainer.

### Stage: Awaiting Invoice Payment
- **Description:** Invoice sent.
- **Action required:** Monitor status until paid.

### Stage: Closed - Won / Lost / Unused
- **Description:** Deal outcome finalised.
- **Action required:** Categorise for reporting and clean-up.

**Tip:** Always refresh the CRM after stage updates to activate automation (for example, quote generation).

## Step 3 - Documentation and Quote Linking

- Ensure all quotes generated are:
  - Appropriately named and include attendee / course / location info.
  - Sent via the CRM email system using approved templates.
- Mark the quote as:
  - "Sent" after delivery.
  - "Closed - Won" after PO or confirmation.
  - "Unused" if not proceeding.
- Use internal notes to track:
  - Client requirements.
  - Objections.
  - Special terms.

## Step 4 - Task Management and Follow-Up

- **After each stage update:**
  - Create a follow-up task (for example, "Follow up quote sent - [Company]").
  - Assign a due date (usually 3–5 business days).
  - Prioritise based on deal value, tier, or urgency.
- **Ongoing task management:**
  - Monitor and update task status (Open, In Progress, Completed).
  - Use the Kanban board view for visibility across all open deals.
- **End of week / month:**
  - Review the pipeline to ensure all deals are in the correct stage.
  - Mark old or inactive deals as "Closed - Lost" or "Unused".
  - Ensure quotes and invoices are matched to deals for reporting.
- Use filters and tags to track:
  - Tier 1 / Tier 2 client deals.
  - Region or industry segments.
  - Proposal pipeline (drafts vs sent vs follow-up).

## Outcomes expected

- Real-time deal visibility across the team.
- Accurate forecasting and reporting.
- Reduced deal leakage or missed follow-ups.
- Consistent quote-to-invoice tracking.
- Higher conversion and improved client retention.
